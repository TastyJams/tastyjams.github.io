Hello perspective CITs!

Becoming a summer camp counselor in training can be an arduous task. A student must know where to go and what to do. My goal as a veteran CIT is to help that student survive the hardships of unpaid camp work.

Rule One: Set rules from the start

Children push so they can find boundaries. If you set your boundaries early, the children won’t push as much. Without borders, you will be tormented constanty.

Rule 2: Be social

While it is important for a CIT’s kids to respect him, it is equally important for the kids to feel comfortable around him. Kids will not open up to a walking rulebook, so socialize.

Rule 3: Understand that the counselors own you

To not go insane during one’s time as a CIT, they must understand that counselors own them. As a CIT, you have no real power, and the only way to get through it is the understanding that one day, you will be a counselor, bossing around your own CITs.

Thanks for Listening!

With my advice heard and considered, I hope all perspective CITs have fun at camp.
